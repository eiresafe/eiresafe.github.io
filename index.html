Emergency Contacts & Support

  • Immediate Danger: If you witness a crime or need urgent assistance, call Garda Emergency Services at 999 or 112.
  • An Garda Síochána Confidential: Share information anonymously about knife crime or assault suspects by calling 1800 666 111.
  • Victim Support at Court (V-SAC): Offering free, confidential court support to victims of crime and their families. Helpline: 01 872 6785.
  • Irish Tourist Assistance Service (ITAS): Free support service for tourists who fall victim to crimes. Call 1890 365 700.

Knife Crime Awareness & Prevention

  • Carry a Weapon, Carry the Risk: Under Irish Law (Firearms and Offensive Weapons Act), carrying a knife in a public place carries a sentence of up to 5 years in prison.
  • De-escalate & Walk Away: If a conflict occurs, immediately walk away. Do not try to resolve disputes where weapons might be present.
  • Parents & Guardians: Have open conversations about safety. Statistics prove that carrying a knife actually increases the likelihood of being injured by one.
